### JobHuntOS — Entrepreneurship & Job Creation

> Autonomous job-search agent: tailors resumes, applies, tracks, and preps every interview.

| BV | AI | CI | First dollar | Solo-build | Wedge | Showcase |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 8 | 9 | 8 | 9 | 9 | 9 | 8 |

Tags: `hr`, `matching`, `doc-automation`, `outbound`

Why it won: near-perfect strategy-lens profile — consumers pay for job-search help immediately (career coaches charge $100s with no software), one builder can ship it, job seekers are the most reachable audience on the internet (r/jobs, LinkedIn, TikTok), and an agent that *visibly applies to jobs and books interviews while you watch* is a strong AI-native demo. Matches Steve's doc-automation + matching edges.

### InterviewGym — Education & Human Potential

> Voice-agent mock interviews with rubric-graded feedback, sold as prep packs to job seekers.

| BV | AI | CI | First dollar | Solo-build | Wedge | Showcase |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 8 | 9 | 7 | 9 | 9 | 8 | 9 |

Tags: `education`, `hr`, `voice`

### Strategic note

Both winners serve the **same customer at the same moment** (an active job seeker), which means they may be one business: JobHuntOS as the operating system, InterviewGym as its highest-margin module and the wedge demo for judges. JobHuntOS sits in Entrepreneurship & Job Creation; InterviewGym in Education & Human Potential — the combined play could credibly enter either category.
