Scoring rubric & how the bracket works

Each idea is scored 1–10 on the three official, equally-weighted Stage-Two criteria. The weighted total (default 1·1·1, max 30) — plus any Founder-Fit bonus and strategy-lens points you've switched on — sets the seed (1 = strongest). Standard 16-team seeding pairs 1v16, 8v9, 5v12, 4v13, 3v14, 6v11, 7v10, 2v15, so the top two seeds can only meet in the final. In each matchup the higher total advances; ties break by Business Viability, then AI-Native Operations, then Category Impact — mirroring the official tie-break order. Every idea clears Stage One (fits a category, uses Google Cloud + Gemini, AI-native, newly built). Each 🎲 draw fields a random 16 of the 32 ideas per category, so re-drawing surfaces different matchups; the simulator aggregates over many draws so the verdict doesn't depend on the luck of one deal.

Business Viability

Real business, real users, real revenue in the 90-day window + sustainability of the model. Favors clear willingness-to-pay, fast time-to-first-dollar, low CAC.

AI-Native Operations

Extent to which AI is live in production and executes key decisions — not a feature, but the operator. Favors agentic workflows doing the actual work.

Category Impact

Meaningfully moves the needle: either redefines how something works, or reaches a scale where widespread adoption is credible.
